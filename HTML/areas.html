Around the United Arab Emirates

Abu Dhabi


Abu Dhabi, the capital of the UAE which also houses the second largest population in the UAE is a major economical and historical hub for the country. The Emirate of Abu Dhabi is the largest in size among the 7 emirates and makes up to 87% of the country’s total area. The Abu Dhabi City itself is an island and is connected to many other islands which boast numerous tourist attractions such as the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, Ferrari World, Yas Water World, the Louvre and much more!

Dubai


Dubai is a major economical hub in the UAE with a focus on trade, tourism, real estate, financial services, and hospitality. It also boasts the largest population of people in the country. Dubai is filled with tourist attracting sites such as the world’s largest building, Burj Khalifa, a 5-star hotel in the Burj Al Arab, the second biggest mall in the Dubai Mall and much more which demonstrates the emirates large wealth and dedication to completing these megaprojects.

Sharjah


Sharjah is the UAE’s third most populated city and is known as the cultural capital of the UAE. Sharjah has numerous Arabic and Islamic architectural sites such as the Heart of Sharjah, Sharjah Art Museum, Sharjah Museum of Islamic Civilization, Al Noor Masjid, and others. There are also many top ranking and beautiful universities present in Sharjah such as the American University of Sharjah and the University of Sharjah.

Ajman


The emirate of Ajman is much like Sharjah because it is in the Dubai-Sharjah-Ajman metropolitan area where the urban area of each emirate flows into the other. Ajman also has a very unique place to visit in the country with the Al Zorah Nature Reserve where you can explore nature in the desert. Additionally, Ajman has many historical landmarks that are very interesting to look at and study about such as Al Murabba Watchtower or the Masfout.

Umm Al Quwain


Umm Al Quwain is very a big contributor to the UAE’s trade and economy as seafood is exported from the ports of the city throughout the Middle East and Europe. In terms of tourism, the emirate has the UAE’s largest water park called the Dreamland Aqua Park and also has numerous recreational activities like skydiving and sailing.

Ras Al Khaimah


Ras Al Khaimah has a very rich history and varied topography which makes it stand out from the rest of the emirates. It has about 1,000 archaeological sites and the highest peak in the UAE with Jebel Jais which has the world’s longest zipline standing 1,934 meters high. The emirate is full of scenic areas such as sand dunes, coastal strips, salt flats, mangroves, and mountains.

Fujairah


Fujairah is a special emirate as it is the only one to be located on the eastern coast of the UAE. Due to this location, Fujairah has a milder climate compared to the heat in Abu Dhabi and Dubai. Fujairah has several hot, cold and mineral springs and one of the most known places in the emirate are the Wadi Al Wurayah waterfalls and Ain Al Madhab Gardens.
